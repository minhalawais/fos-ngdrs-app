**Subject: Mandatory Reporting & Standardised Data Submission on GBV and Technology-Facilitated GBV (TFGBV)**

The National Commission on the Status of Women (NCSW), under its mandate as per the NCSW Act 2012, is responsible for reviewing laws,
policies, mechanisms and practices affecting the status and rights of women and to monitor implementation of Pakistan‚Äôs international
commitments including CEDAW, EU GSP+ conventions, SDG 5, and relevant ILO instruments.

In order to strengthen evidence-based policy decisions, enhance compliance reporting, and support coordinated prevention and response to
gender-based violence (GBV) and technology-facilitated GBV (TFGBV), **all provincial and federal entities are hereby directed** to implement
standardised GBV/TFGBV case reporting and share data with NCSW in the prescribed format.

## I. Mandatory Data Reporting Requirements

The following shall be reported using the attached templates:

1. **Reported Incidents** ‚Äì All GBV & TFGBV cases registered (FIR and non-FIR)
2. **Case Progression** ‚Äì Investigation, prosecution, conviction, closure status
3. **Service Provision** ‚Äì Medical, legal, psychosocial, shelter, digital forensics
4. **Platform/Data Requests (TFGBV)** ‚Äì Takedown requests, response times, evidence usability
5. **Geospatial Information** ‚Äì District-wise mapping, high-risk areas, recurrence profiles

All data shall be **disaggregated by** : age group, disability status, district, location-type, perpetrator-type, and platform (for TFGBV).

# 3 Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

## SOP 1: Case Intake & Registration

1. Survivor encounter documented within **24 hours**
2. Assign **Case_ID (unique)** in provincial system
3. Fill complaint form ‚Üí digital entry to master sheet
4. Category tagging using **standard codes**
5. Survivor safety & confidentiality protocol activated

## SOP 2: Classification & Coding

```
‚óè All cases MUST be classified using GBV/TFGBV Codebook
‚óè If multiple offences ‚Üí record primary + associated type
‚óè Digital evidence checklist for TFGBV:
o Screenshots
o Metadata
o URL/time stamps
o Hash copies
o Platform complaint number
```
## SOP 3: Data Triangulation

Before submission, district focal point must verify with:


```
‚óè Police records
‚óè Shelter/medical services
‚óè Prosecution database
‚óè Cybercrime reports
```
Missing data ‚Üí flagged and sent back to origin office within **72 hours**

## SOP 4: Confidentiality & Ethical Handling

```
‚óè No personally identifiable information (PII) sent to NCSW
‚óè Case references used for audit access only
‚óè Red-zone districts ‚Üí send anonymized trend summaries
```
## SOP 5: Submission & Quality Control

**Step Responsible Party Validation**
Data Entry District focal point Format & coding check

Provincial Review WDD / Police Internal reconciliation
Submission Provincial focal point Certification
NCSW Intake Data Unit Integrity & duplication check
